Learning

My favorite part of computer science is that there is always more to learn. In the interest of this, I’m listing some of the projects I’ve made that I used as learning exercises. While it makes me cringe a little to look at my previous skills, it also excites me to see how far I have come and how far I have yet to go.

The Loot Brothers is a website I made for a joke and practice. It is the first website I made and let me apply my newly earned frontend knowledge. It is based on a fictional medieval company in a Dungeons and Dragons campaign.

The Triangle Auto Solver website is an online application I created during a Pre-Calc class to check the multitude of "solve the triangle" problems we had to do. Enter any three givens of a triangle and the calculator will give you the other three.

Character Tracker is a web application made to keep track of base statistics in a group of players in a Dungeons and Dragons campaign. To make this possible, I used Javascript ES6 for the first time. It includes functionality to dynamically add players and their stats to the page, as well as saving and loading them from browser storage.
